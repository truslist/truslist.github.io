TrusList

An Open and Trustworthy Domain Ranking System

This repository presents **TrusList**, a novel framework designed to overcome the credibility issues of existing domain top lists. By incorporating a multi-dimensional evaluation, we aim for **higher stability, broader coverage, and better retention of regionally significant domains.**

Abstract & Motivation

Domain top lists are fundamental datasets for Internet measurement, security analysis, and Web ecosystem studies. However, their credibility is frequently compromised by susceptibility to manipulation, opaque ranking criteria, and geographic biases. To overcome these issues, this paper proposes **TrusList**, an open and trustworthy domain ranking system based on a multi-dimensional evaluation framework.

Our Multi-dimensional Approach

Beyond Conventional Metrics

Extends beyond conventional traffic metrics by incorporating spatio-temporal characteristics of domain queries, inter-domain dependencies, and the reputation of registrars and top-level domains.

Enhanced Reliability

Further reliability through cross-validation and integration with large-scale passive DNS data and existing domain lists, leveraging multi-source complementarity to mitigate manipulation and single-source bias.

Superior Performance

Extensive evaluation demonstrates that TrusList achieves broader coverage, higher stability, and better retention of regionally significant domains compared to existing alternatives.

Practical Utility & Case Studies

The practical utility of TrusList is illustrated through compelling case studies on **DGA domain detection**, showcasing its effectiveness in real-world security analysis scenarios. Our framework provides a robust foundation for more reliable and unbiased internet research.
